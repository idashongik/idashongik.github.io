From Director

Prof. David Hall

June. 2023

More Human than Human

Philip K Dick

The ongoing revolution of AI ( and AGI ) will only be a problem for those artists and designers whose work is formulaic and an aggregation. Soon finally the only ones left will be the actual human creatives. Searching and analysing the internet for inspiration is what the present day AIs do very well, and as such those behaviours we can not compete with.

Like photography allowed painters to be free of the constraints of realism, AI will ( and has already started ) make the vast majority of lawyers, coders , photographers and engineers redundant. Many design disciplines have already started to feel this process.

The great reset is not what we thought it was, it is something much more insideous. So this is our task, and our method of survival. Identify what was lost as human creatives. The so called AI apocalypse may seem terrifying but we see it as an opportunity.

The exhibition

Why vertical screens in portrait format? This is an emulation of an oversized telephone interface. Its the primary method that people engage and interact with the internet.

Why Projectors?

This is a kind escape from the present day hand held device. The images in our exhibition look more augmented, images in space.

Whats the abstract animation and projection? This is an transformative augmented reality display. In the daytime it looks flat and sculptural and in the evening transforms. Even when the gallery is inaccessible the passersby can engage with the exhibition.
